Belly Button Biodiversity Dashboard

Use the interactive charts below to explore the dataset

Is your navel home to a unique community of microorganisms?

This dashboard presents data from 153 individuals whose belly buttons were sampled for bacteria. Operational taxon units (OTUs) were identified from all individuals to characterize the microbial community in their navels and identify potential species useful for synthetic meat production.

Use the dropdown menu below to search through anonymized results. Individual results include the top 10 most common OTUs, the frequency with which the subject washed their navel, and the counts of all identified OTUs.
